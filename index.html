▾
Purpose (FIT Documentation): Humming challenges the right cortex; counting challenges the left cortex. A previously strong arm should remain strong during each activity. If the arm goes weak, it indicates that cortex cannot keep up with the demands placed on it. Combined with other FIT findings, this provides a deeper understanding of neurological imbalances in the Brain/Body Map.

FIT — Cerebellum / Progressive Balance
Romberg → Airex Pad → March in Place → Sobriety Test
~2 min
▾
Purpose (FIT Documentation): With eyes open, three sensory systems — vision, proprioception, and vestibular sense — provide input to the cerebellum to maintain trunk and body stability. Removing visual input (eyes closed) reveals vestibular or proprioceptive system lesions. A midline cerebellar lesion causing truncal instability will produce instability even with eyes open. The progressive difficulty of this test maps cerebellar function systematically.

~3 min
▾
Purpose (FIT Documentation): Band Extensions may identify unknown imbalances in the nervous system and/or musculature contributing to anatomical distortion of the neck and shoulder area. Voluntary motion of the active arm involves the same-side cerebellum and opposite-side parietal lobe. Stabilization involves the same-side parietal lobe. Side-to-side asymmetry in this test reveals neurological imbalances that contribute to Upper Cross Syndrome patterns, and is part of the 3D Brain/Body Map.

13
FIT — Standing Stress Test (SI Ligament)
Serola Sacral Belt — Most missed joint in the body
~1 min
▾
Purpose (FIT Documentation): Used to identify if the person has a sprained SI ligament and if a Sacro-Iliac belt would benefit them. The sacroiliac ligament is the most weight-bearing and the largest ligament in the body. SI injuries are the most missed and mismanaged joint in the body. A positive test can affect not only low back but also carpal tunnel, the brain, shoulder, and plantar fasciitis. Note: instruct the patient to choose which leg to stand on first — they will intuitively stand on their stronger side.
